🌍 Countries That Commonly Use Sauerkraut

Sauerkraut (fermented cabbage) is popular across many parts of the world — especially in countries where traditional diets value natural preservation and gut health.

🇩🇪

Germany

The word sauerkraut actually comes from German, meaning "sour cabbage." It's a national dish often served with sausages, meats, and potatoes.

🇷🇺

Russia

Known as kvashenaya kapusta, eaten daily in winter as a source of vitamins when fresh vegetables are scarce.

🇵🇱

Poland

Used in traditional dishes like bigos (hunter's stew) and pierogi fillings.

🇨🇭

Switzerland & Austria

Commonly served with roasts and sausages; considered a winter immune booster.

🇫🇷

France (Alsace)

Known as choucroute, often paired with meats and wines.

🇨🇳

China

A similar fermented cabbage called suan cai has been eaten for over 2,000 years.

🇰🇷

Korea

The Korean version, kimchi, is one of the most famous global ferments made from cabbage, radish, and spices.

🇺🇸

United States

Popular as a probiotic food and used in sandwiches (like Reuben) and salads.
